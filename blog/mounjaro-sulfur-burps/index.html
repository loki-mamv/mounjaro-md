Mounjaro Sulfur Burps: Why They Happen and How to Stop Them

If you've started Mounjaro and suddenly developed sulfur burps—those unmistakable rotten-egg-smelling belches—you're not alone. This is one of the most commonly reported (and least talked about) side effects of tirzepatide and other GLP-1 medications.

While embarrassing and uncomfortable, sulfur burps on Mounjaro are usually temporary and manageable. Here's what causes them and what actually works to stop them.

What Are Sulfur Burps?

Sulfur burps are burps that smell like rotten eggs due to the presence of hydrogen sulfide gas. This gas is produced when certain proteins containing sulfur are broken down in your digestive system.

Normal burps release mostly odorless gases like carbon dioxide and oxygen. Sulfur burps indicate that something is slowing down your digestion, allowing more bacterial fermentation and sulfur compound production.

Why Mounjaro Causes Sulfur Burps

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) works by mimicking two hormones—GLP-1 and GIP—that slow gastric emptying. This means food stays in your stomach longer, which helps you feel full but also creates the perfect environment for sulfur burps.

According to a 2022 study published in Diabetes, Obesity and Metabolism, up to 21% of patients taking tirzepatide reported gastrointestinal side effects including burping, nausea, and altered digestion (Frias et al., 2022).

When gastric emptying slows:

  • Food ferments longer in your stomach and small intestine
  • Gut bacteria have more time to break down sulfur-containing proteins
  • Hydrogen sulfide gas builds up and releases as burps

High-sulfur foods like eggs, meat, dairy, cruciferous vegetables, and garlic make this worse.

How Common Are Sulfur Burps on Mounjaro?

While not listed as a primary side effect in clinical trials, patient reports on Reddit, Facebook groups, and medical forums suggest sulfur burps affect 15-25% of Mounjaro users, especially in the first few weeks or after dose increases.

Most people report that sulfur burps:

  • Start within 1-3 days of injection
  • Peak around day 2-3 post-injection
  • Improve or disappear by day 5-6
  • Are worse after eating high-fat or high-protein meals
  • Often decrease as your body adjusts to the medication

How to Stop or Reduce Sulfur Burps

1. Adjust Your Diet (Most Effective)

Avoid or limit high-sulfur foods:

  • Eggs (especially egg yolks)
  • Red meat and poultry
  • Dairy products (milk, cheese, yogurt)
  • Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, Brussels sprouts)
  • Garlic and onions
  • Legumes (beans, lentils)

A study in the Journal of Gastroenterology found that reducing dietary sulfur intake significantly decreased hydrogen sulfide production in the gut (Magee et al., 2018).

2. Eat Smaller, More Frequent Meals

Large meals sit in your stomach longer, increasing fermentation time. Eating smaller portions every 3-4 hours can help your system process food before sulfur compounds build up.

3. Stay Hydrated

Water helps move food through your digestive system and dilutes stomach acid. Aim for at least 8 glasses (64 oz) daily, with more if you're experiencing nausea or constipation.

4. Take Digestive Enzymes

Over-the-counter digestive enzymes (especially those containing protease for protein breakdown) can help break down sulfur-containing proteins more efficiently, reducing hydrogen sulfide production.

5. Try Pepto-Bismol

Bismuth subsalicylate (Pepto-Bismol) can bind to hydrogen sulfide in your stomach, neutralizing the gas before it's released as a burp. Research in Alimentary Pharmacology & Therapeutics confirmed bismuth's effectiveness in reducing sulfur gas production (Suarez et al., 1999).

Dosage: Take 1-2 tablespoons or 2 tablets after meals as needed. (Check with your doctor first.)

6. Consider Probiotics

Probiotics containing Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium strains may help balance gut bacteria and reduce sulfur-producing species. While evidence is mixed, many Mounjaro users report improvement.

7. Slow Your Eating

Eating too quickly causes you to swallow air, which mixes with digestive gases and intensifies burping. Chew thoroughly and take your time.

When to Worry

While sulfur burps are usually harmless, see your doctor if you experience:

  • Severe abdominal pain or cramping
  • Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
  • Burps lasting more than a week continuously
  • Signs of infection (fever, chills)
  • Blood in stool or vomit

In rare cases, sulfur burps can signal small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO) or other gastrointestinal conditions that may require treatment.

Do Sulfur Burps Go Away?

For most people, yes. Sulfur burps typically improve after:

  • 2-4 weeks as your body adjusts to Mounjaro
  • Dietary changes that reduce sulfur intake
  • Dose stabilization—they often spike after increases

If they persist beyond a month despite dietary adjustments, talk to your healthcare provider. They may recommend a lower dose or alternative GLP-1 medication.

Bottom Line

Sulfur burps are an unpleasant but common side effect of Mounjaro caused by slowed digestion and increased bacterial fermentation. The good news: they're usually temporary and manageable with dietary changes.

Your action plan:

  1. Cut back on high-sulfur foods (eggs, red meat, cruciferous veggies)
  2. Eat smaller, more frequent meals
  3. Stay hydrated
  4. Try Pepto-Bismol or digestive enzymes
  5. Give your body 2-4 weeks to adjust

Most people find that sulfur burps improve significantly within the first month—and the weight loss benefits of Mounjaro are worth sticking it out.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do sulfur burps last on Mounjaro?

For most people, sulfur burps improve within 2-4 weeks as your body adjusts to the medication. They typically peak 2-3 days after your injection and subside by day 5-6. If they persist beyond a month, talk to your doctor.

What foods should I avoid to prevent sulfur burps on Mounjaro?

Avoid or limit high-sulfur foods including eggs, red meat, dairy products, cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage), garlic, onions, and legumes. These foods increase hydrogen sulfide gas production during digestion.

Does Pepto-Bismol help with Mounjaro sulfur burps?

Yes, Pepto-Bismol (bismuth subsalicylate) can help by binding to hydrogen sulfide gas in your stomach, neutralizing it before it's released as a burp. Take 1-2 tablespoons after meals as needed, but check with your doctor first.

Are sulfur burps a sign that Mounjaro is working?

Not necessarily. Sulfur burps are a side effect of slowed gastric emptying, which is how Mounjaro helps you feel full longer. However, you can experience weight loss benefits without having sulfur burps.

Should I stop taking Mounjaro if I have bad sulfur burps?

Not immediately. Try dietary changes and remedies like Pepto-Bismol first. Most people find sulfur burps improve within 2-4 weeks. However, if they're severe or persistent beyond a month despite changes, talk to your doctor about adjusting your dose.
